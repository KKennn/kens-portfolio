KID MENTOR

helps children overcome electronics addiction by setting plans to follow and earning rewards.

01 Research

Interview with parents and kids

I interviewed 10 parents who were concerned about their children's overusing electronic devices. I also interviewed their kids.

Parents said,

"My kids always try to violate rules of using iPad."

"I want my kid to have a fruitful childhood."

"I hardly have any time or methods to manage my kid’s life."

Children said,

"I like playing video games for a long time."

"I know the benefits of keeping a regular life but sometimes I just can’t control myself."

"My plan is often disrupted because I want to play video games."

Shadowing

Setting a time limit is a common parental control over electronic devices. I shadowed 5 children and saw how they responded to it.

02 Analyze

Persona

The interview and shadowing show: Kids don't respond well to game time limit. They want to finish homework and plans but they procrastinate because of addiction to games. I sorted out characteristics, current solutions, and demands of target users.

Storyboard

I illustrated the pain point: Kids try to have a plan and control their usage of electronic devices, but their lack of self-management skills restrains them.

Problem Tree

I defined causes and effects of the core problem: the overuse of electronic devices.

03 Brainstorm

I brainstormed three concepts to address the pain point here. After evaluation, I chose to design an interactive box that prints “mission cards” for children to "clock out" after completing plans. It also regulates iPad usage with an app.

04 Create

Workflow & Wireframe

Set up a timetable
→Mission card printed at scheduled time
→Clock out after completing the "mission"
→Unlock game time!
